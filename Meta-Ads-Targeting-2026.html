Meta Ads Targeting 2026: Broad vs Interest vs Lookalike: When to Use Each

The question is not which targeting method is best: it is which is right for your account's signal volume, budget level, and objective type. This guide gives you the exact decision framework with UK and India data for every targeting type.

Executive Summary
  • Broad targeting outperforms interest targeting for ecommerce accounts with 50+ weekly conversions and CAPI active. Andromeda finds buyers in large pools more efficiently than stacked interests.
  • Interest targeting is not dead: it is context-dependent. B2B, regulated industries, and lower-budget accounts consistently benefit from interest guardrails.
  • Lookalike audiences require quality source data. A 1% lookalike from verified purchasers outperforms a 1% lookalike from page engagers. Source quality determines output quality.
  • Custom audiences are the retargeting foundation: delivering 40–60% lower CPL than cold prospecting in every account Advertza manages.
  • The conditions must exist for broad to work. Without CAPI and 50+ weekly conversions, broad targeting produces diffuse, inefficient results.

What iOS 14.5 Changed About Targeting

Before April 2021, Meta's targeting precision relied heavily on cross-site tracking data. iOS 14.5 ended this for a large and growing segment. iPhone users who selected "Ask App Not to Track" removed themselves from cross-app tracking. Meta lost the signal that made detailed interest targeting most valuable.

The practical impact has two dimensions. First, interest targeting categories became less accurate: relying more on self-reported profile data and in-platform engagement rather than third-party browsing behaviour. Second, Meta's algorithm lost a portion of the conversion feedback signal that allowed it to self-optimise within interest stacks.

What iOS 14.5 did NOT kill: Custom audiences built from your customer email list are completely unaffected by iOS. Your first-party data: email lists, phone number lists, CRM exports: is the most iOS-proof targeting asset you own.

Broad Targeting: When It Wins

Broad Targeting Wins When Three Conditions Are Met Simultaneously
  • The account generates 50 or more purchase or lead events per week
  • CAPI is active and providing server-side conversion data
  • The creative is specific enough to self-select the right audience: a specific problem/solution creative repels non-buyers and concentrates engagement among buyers, doing the targeting work that interest stacks once did

In accounts Advertza manages meeting all three conditions, switching from interest stacks to broad typically produces CPM reductions of 7–12% and conversion volume increases of 15–25% within 30 days.

A UK D2C supplement brand had been running interest-stacked prospecting for 14 months. We ran a 30-day split: 50% budget on existing interest targeting, 50% on pure broad (UK, 18–55, no interests). The account had CAPI active and averaged 65 purchases per week. Result: Broad achieved 27% lower CPM and 29% lower CPL. Interest campaigns were paused in month 2.

Interest Targeting: 4 Scenarios Where It Still Wins

1. Accounts under the conversion threshold. If you generate fewer than 50 conversions per week, broad targeting lacks the signal to exit learning phase. Interest targeting provides structure while the account builds volume.

2. B2B advertisers with specific buyer profiles. Broad targeting has no mechanism for buyer specificity like "procurement managers at manufacturing companies with 50–200 employees." Job function, employer size, and industry targeting layers remain the most effective approach. B2B decision-makers spend 74% more time on Facebook than average users: but reaching specifically the right ones requires targeting structure.

3. Regulated categories. Finance, healthcare, credit, and employment advertisers face restrictions on detailed targeting parameters. Within these constraints, the remaining eligible interest layers provide useful audience guardrails.

4. New accounts with no pixel history. An account with zero conversion history has nothing for Andromeda to learn from. Interest targeting provides delivery direction while the account builds its first 30 days of pixel data.

Always exclude your existing customer list from prospecting campaigns. If you are running a broad prospecting campaign without excluding purchasers, a portion of your prospecting budget is targeting people who have already bought from you. This wastes spend and pollutes your prospecting CPL data.

Lookalike Audiences: Best Practices for 2026

  • Source quality beats source size: A lookalike from 2,000 verified purchasers outperforms one from 10,000 email subscribers who never bought
  • 1% for prospecting, 2–3% when 1% saturates: Start with 1% lookalike. When frequency exceeds 3.5 and CTR drops, expand to 2–3%
  • Monthly refresh: Re-upload your source list monthly. New customers are the freshest signal
  • UK and India separately: Create separate lookalike audiences for each market: combining markets in a single source degrades both

Should I use broad or interest targeting in 2026?+
For ecommerce accounts generating 50 or more purchases per week with CAPI active, broad targeting consistently outperforms interest-stacked targeting in 2026. Meta's Andromeda algorithm finds converting users within large audience pools more efficiently than narrow interest constraints allow. For accounts below the 50-purchases-per-week threshold, interest targeting with 2–3 stacked layers provides guardrails while building signal volume. B2B advertisers, regulated categories, and lower-budget accounts all benefit from interest targeting as a starting structure. The answer depends on your signal volume.
How do I create a lookalike audience on Meta?+
In Ads Manager, go to Audiences → Create Audience → Lookalike Audience. Select a source custom audience: your customer purchase list is the highest-quality source. Set the country, then the audience size as a percentage (1% is most recommended for prospecting). You need a minimum of 2,000 source records for a reliable 1% lookalike. Use your actual purchasers as the source, not email subscribers or page followers. Update the source list monthly. Create separate lookalikes for UK and India: combining markets in one source degrades both.
What happened to detailed targeting after iOS 14.5?+
iOS 14.5 reduced the quality of Meta's inferred interest and behaviour data by removing cross-app tracking for opted-out iPhone users. Interest categories now rely more heavily on self-reported profile data and in-platform engagement rather than third-party browsing behaviour. First-party custom audiences: built from your email lists and website events: are entirely unaffected because they do not use browser tracking. If you are not uploading your customer list monthly, you are leaving the most iOS-proof and highest-quality targeting signals unused.
What is the minimum audience size for a Meta lookalike?+
Meta requires 100 source records to generate a lookalike, but you should not build one from fewer than 2,000 records. Below 2,000, the statistical model lacks diversity: it over-fits to the narrow characteristics of your limited data. Between 2,000 and 50,000 source records, quality improves incrementally with list size. Create separate lookalikes for each country you advertise in: a UK purchaser profile is statistically different from an India purchaser profile.
