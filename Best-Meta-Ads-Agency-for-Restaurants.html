Restaurant Meta Ads Challenges
  • Proximity is everything. A restaurant ad served to someone 20 miles away wastes budget. Geographic precision is the primary targeting lever: getting radius sizing right (3–7 miles dine-in, up to 10 miles delivery) is the foundation of efficient spend.
  • Competing with aggregators. Deliveroo, Just Eat, and Uber Eats run significant Meta budgets promoting restaurants on their platforms. Direct restaurant campaigns must compete with platforms that have already converted the customer once.
  • Low average order value. With UK restaurant orders at £25–£40 dine-in and £18–£30 delivery, the margin available for customer acquisition is thin: CPL discipline is non-negotiable.
  • Creative fatigue. Food photography feels ubiquitous on social feeds: the differentiator is authentic, behind-the-scenes, preparation video content that stands out from polished food imagery.

Food Video: The Highest-Performing Creative for Restaurants

Short food content (15–30s, 9:16) showing dish preparation, plating, or dining atmosphere drives 3× higher engagement than static menu images. Reels CPM averages £9.50 UK: 12% below Instagram Feed: making it the most cost-efficient placement for food video. Shot on smartphone for authentic, native feel. Open with the most visually compelling moment of the dish: the cheese pull, the sauce pour, the final plating. Add text overlay with key details (dish name, dine-in or delivery CTA).

Meta Ads Strategy for Restaurants

Four-Part Restaurant Campaign Structure

Geo-radius prospecting: 3–5 mile radius for dine-in, 5–10 miles for delivery. Age 18–45 primary demographic layer. Food video on Reels placement (lowest CPM for food content).

7-day menu visitor retargeting: Website visitors who viewed the menu but did not book or order. This audience converts at 60–70% lower CPL than cold prospecting. Simple "ready to order?" CTA with the most popular dish visual.

Booking Lead Ads: For reservation-based restaurants, Lead Ads with date/time/party size fields achieve higher booking rates than outbound links to reservation pages on mobile. Direct connection to OpenTable or ResDiary via webhook.

Delivery conversion tracking: For direct delivery, set up Purchase event on order confirmation with order value parameter. CAPI via website integration. Optimise campaigns for Purchase at above-breakeven ROAS.

What is the average Meta Ads CPL for restaurants in the UK?+

How does Advertza structure Meta Ads campaigns?+
Advertza builds every Meta Ads account on a three-campaign architecture aligned with Meta's 50-events-per-week learning phase requirement: a broad prospecting campaign (60–70% of budget) using UGC-style 9:16 video, a warm-audience retargeting campaign (20–25%) using testimonials and offer-focused creative, and an Advantage+ Shopping Campaign (15–20%) for accounts generating 50+ weekly purchases. This structure prevents budget fragmentation and gives each campaign sufficient signal to exit the learning phase and stabilise performance.
Why is CAPI essential for Meta Ads in 2026?+
CAPI (Conversions API) sends conversion events directly from your server to Meta, bypassing iOS tracking restrictions that block browser-based pixel data. Pixel-only accounts miss 30–55% of actual conversions in 2026. CAPI recovers an average 15% more attributed conversions, giving Meta's algorithm the complete data it needs to optimise delivery. Without CAPI, every campaign decision: bidding, audience selection, creative prioritisation: is made on incomplete information. Advertza implements CAPI in Week 1 of every engagement.
Should restaurants use Meta Ads or Google Ads?+
Both serve different demand stages. Google captures active search intent ('restaurants near me', 'pizza delivery [city]'): high intent but smaller audience. Meta creates demand among nearby users not actively searching: lower CPL, broader local reach, and stronger for brand awareness, special offers, and event bookings (Valentine's Day, Christmas, Diwali). The best restaurant marketing uses Google for search-intent capture and Meta for local awareness and event campaigns.
What Meta Ads creative works best for restaurants?+
Food video is the highest-performing creative format: short-form video (15–30 seconds) showing food preparation, plating, or the dining experience produces 3× higher engagement than static photography. Reels format benefits from lower CPM (£9.50 avg UK) and organic-like presentation. For delivery campaigns, before/after reveal videos and social proof content consistently outperform standard food photography. Authenticity and genuine behind-the-scenes content outperforms polished editorial photography.
