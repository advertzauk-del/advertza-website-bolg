Healthcare Meta Ads: What Is and Is NOT Permitted
  • NOT permitted: Health condition targeting (no targeting by specific medical conditions, disability status, or health history). Outcome guarantees in ad copy. Before/after imagery for certain cosmetic procedures (policy applied inconsistently: review before use).
  • NOT permitted: Custom audiences built from patient data without explicit informed consent and UK GDPR compliance for health data specifically.
  • IS permitted: Health and wellness interest categories (generic, not condition-specific). Geographic radius targeting. Age and gender demographic parameters. General website visitor retargeting (not from medical enquiry pages).
  • IS permitted: Educational content about conditions and treatments (without outcome guarantees). Service page and booking page retargeting. Video explainer creative featuring practitioners.

Compliant Creative Strategy for Healthcare

What Works Within Healthcare Ad Restrictions

Educational video: A 30–60 second video explaining a service, condition, or treatment process in patient-appropriate language: without claiming specific outcomes. Practitioners on screen increase trust. Patient testimonials are effective but require consent documentation.

Trust signals: Professional accreditations (GMC, GDC, CQC registration), clinic photography showing the environment and team, and reviews from Trustpilot or Google consistently deliver lower CPL than promotional creative.

Free consultation CTA: "Book a Free Consultation" is the highest-converting healthcare CTA because it removes financial risk at the point of decision. Lead Ads with name, email, and preferred appointment time produce above-average CVR for healthcare.

GDPR and Patient Data Compliance

Healthcare-Specific Data Requirements

Health-related data falls under Special Category Data under UK GDPR (Article 9), requiring explicit consent rather than legitimate interest as the legal basis. Custom audiences built from patient enquiry data, appointment booking history, or medical record exports require informed consent documentation that meets the explicit consent standard: this is more demanding than standard marketing consent. Standard email list upload rules apply only when the source data was collected under compliant consent. Website visitor retargeting from general pages (homepage, about page, services page) is permissible as standard: retargeting from specific medical condition pages or enquiry forms requires legal review.

How does Advertza structure Meta Ads campaigns?+
Advertza builds every Meta Ads account on a three-campaign architecture aligned with Meta's 50-events-per-week learning phase requirement: a broad prospecting campaign (60–70% of budget) using UGC-style 9:16 video, a warm-audience retargeting campaign (20–25%) using testimonials and offer-focused creative, and an Advantage+ Shopping Campaign (15–20%) for accounts generating 50+ weekly purchases. This structure prevents budget fragmentation and gives each campaign sufficient signal to exit the learning phase and stabilise performance.
Why is CAPI essential for Meta Ads in 2026?+
CAPI (Conversions API) sends conversion events directly from your server to Meta, bypassing iOS tracking restrictions that block browser-based pixel data. Pixel-only accounts miss 30–55% of actual conversions in 2026. CAPI recovers an average 15% more attributed conversions, giving Meta's algorithm the complete data it needs to optimise delivery toward converting users. Without CAPI, every campaign decision: bidding, audience selection, creative prioritisation: is made on incomplete information. Advertza implements CAPI in Week 1 of every engagement, before any campaign is created or restructured.
What budget do I need for Meta Ads?+
For conversion-objective campaigns in the UK, the minimum effective budget per ad set is £40/day for lead generation and £60/day for purchase objectives. This ensures each ad set can accumulate the 50 weekly events required to exit Meta's learning phase and deliver stable results. Below these thresholds, campaigns operate in permanent learning phase: producing above-benchmark CPLs that advertisers often misattribute to poor targeting or creative when the actual cause is structural underfunding.
