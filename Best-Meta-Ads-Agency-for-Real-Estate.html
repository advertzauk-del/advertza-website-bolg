Real Estate Meta Ads Challenges
  • Distinguishing serious buyers. Meta's broad audience reach includes many users who will enquire but never view. Filtering at the ad and form level is essential.
  • High-value, low-volume transactions. A single property sale may involve 15–20 viewings and a multi-month sales cycle: optimising for lead volume without lead quality produces poor ROI.
  • 90-day consideration cycles. A buyer who sees your ad in January may not enquire until March: standard 30-day retargeting windows lose these high-intent prospects.
  • Compliance. Property advertising is subject to financial promotion rules where mortgage or investment claims are made: creative must be reviewed before publishing.

Video Walk-Through: The Quality Differentiator

Video property tours in 9:16 vertical format produce 60% higher lead quality than static image ads: because buyers who complete a video have pre-qualified themselves through engagement. A 15–30 second walk-through that shows key rooms, the exterior, and the surrounding area filters casual browsers before they reach the enquiry form. The result is fewer total enquiries but a significantly higher appointment-to-enquiry ratio: the metric that actually matters for estate agent conversion economics.

Lead Quality: The Qualifying Form Strategy

Qualifying Questions That Filter Serious Buyers
  • What is your current situation? (Renting / Own and selling / First-time buyer / Buy-to-let investor)
  • When are you looking to move? (Within 3 months / 3–6 months / 6–12 months / Just looking)
  • Have you spoken to a mortgage broker? (Yes / No: removes financially unqualified leads)
  • What is your budget range? (Segmented by property type and local market)

Leads who complete four qualifying questions are significantly more likely to attend viewings than leads from unqualified forms. The friction cost is a lower form completion rate: which is a feature, not a bug, when the goal is appointment quality.

How does Advertza structure Meta Ads campaigns?+
Advertza builds every Meta Ads account on a three-campaign architecture aligned with Meta's 50-events-per-week learning phase requirement: a broad prospecting campaign (60–70% of budget) using UGC-style 9:16 video, a warm-audience retargeting campaign (20–25%) using testimonials and offer-focused creative, and an Advantage+ Shopping Campaign (15–20%) for accounts generating 50+ weekly purchases. Why is CAPI essential for Meta Ads in 2026?+
CAPI (Conversions API) sends conversion events directly from your server to Meta, bypassing iOS tracking restrictions that block browser-based pixel data. Pixel-only accounts miss 30–55% of actual conversions in 2026. CAPI recovers an average 15% more attributed conversions, giving Meta's algorithm the complete data it needs to optimise delivery toward converting users. Without CAPI, every campaign decision: bidding, audience selection, creative prioritisation: is made on incomplete information. Advertza implements CAPI in Week 1 of every engagement, before any campaign is created or restructured.
